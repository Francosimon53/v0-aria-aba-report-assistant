# Discharge and Transition Planning

## Discharge Criteria

### Mastery-Based Discharge
1. All treatment goals met
2. Skills generalized across settings/people
3. Skills maintained over time
4. Caregivers competent to support progress
5. Adaptive functioning approaching normal limits

### Plateau-Based Discharge
- No progress for 6+ months despite modifications
- Multiple treatment modifications attempted
- Client at maximum potential for current goals

### Administrative Discharge
- Family relocating
- Insurance/funding changes
- Family request
- Safety concerns

---

## Transition Planning

### Intensive to Focused ABA

Hour Reduction Schedule:
- 30-40 hrs → 25-30 hrs (4-6 weeks)
- 25-30 hrs → 20-25 hrs (4-6 weeks)
- 20-25 hrs → 15-20 hrs (4-6 weeks)
- 15-20 hrs → 10-15 hrs (ongoing)

Monitor:
- Skill maintenance
- Behavior regression
- Family stress
- School functioning

### Transition to School

**6 Months Before:**
- Contact school district
- Schedule transition meeting
- Teach classroom readiness skills

**3 Months Before:**
- Visit target school
- Meet teacher
- Share assessments
- Develop IEP goals

**1 Month Before:**
- Finalize IEP
- Train school staff
- Create visual supports
- Practice school routines

**Documents for School:**
- Assessment results
- Mastered skills and current targets
- Effective reinforcers
- Behavior intervention plan
- Communication system
- Sensory needs

### Transition to Consultation Model

From: Direct 1:1 ABA
To: Monthly BCBA consultation + caregiver implementation

Structure:
- 2-4 hours BCBA consultation monthly
- Caregiver-implemented programming
- Periodic direct assessment
- Data review and updates
- Crisis consultation available

## Transfer to New Provider

**Documents to Transfer:**
- Recent assessments
- Current treatment plan
- Behavior Intervention Plan
- 6 months progress data
- Session note summary
- Caregiver training documentation
- Reinforcer assessment
- Communication system info

**Information to Provide:**
- What works (reinforcers, strategies)
- What doesn't work
- Current skill levels
- Behavior triggers and functions
- Caregiver training status
- Medical/sensory considerations
