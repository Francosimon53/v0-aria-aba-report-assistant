# TRICARE ABA Therapy Coverage Requirements

## Overview

TRICARE covers Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) for beneficiaries diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Coverage is provided under the TRICARE Comprehensive Autism Care Demonstration (ACD).

## Eligibility Requirements

### Who is Covered:
- Active duty family members
- Retirees and their families
- Survivors
- Beneficiaries under age 21 (may extend to 26 in some cases)

### Diagnosis Requirements:
- DSM-5 diagnosis: Autism Spectrum Disorder (F84.0)
- Diagnosis by qualified professional (psychologist, psychiatrist, developmental pediatrician)
- Comprehensive diagnostic evaluation within past 12 months

## Authorization Process

### No Prior Authorization Required
TRICARE does not require prior authorization for ABA services. However, providers must:
- Be TRICARE-authorized ABA providers
- Submit accurate claims
- Maintain proper documentation
- Follow BACB guidelines

### Provider Enrollment:
- Complete TRICARE provider enrollment
- Maintain BCBA certification
- Obtain National Provider Identifier (NPI)
- Complete required credentialing

## Assessment Requirements

### Initial Comprehensive Assessment Must Include:

1. **Diagnostic Evaluation**
   - ADOS-2 (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule)
   - ADI-R (Autism Diagnostic Interview - Revised) or equivalent
   - Cognitive assessment
   - DSM-5 diagnostic criteria documentation

2. **Functional Assessment**
   - Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales (VABS-3)
   - Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA)
   - Baseline data across all domains
   - Environmental assessment

3. **Skill Assessment**
   - Communication abilities
   - Social skills
   - Academic/pre-academic skills
   - Daily living skills
   - Motor skills

## Treatment Plan Requirements

### Individualized Treatment Plan Must Include:

1. **Client Information**
   - Demographics
   - Diagnosis
   - Current skill levels
   - Strengths and challenges

2. **Treatment Goals**
   - Minimum 3-5 goals across domains
   - SMART format (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
   - Based on assessment results
   - Prioritized by family input

3. **Intervention Strategies**
   - Evidence-based procedures
   - Teaching methods
   - Reinforcement strategies
   - Generalization plans

4. **Service Delivery Plan**
   - Recommended hours per week (1:1 and group)
   - Service setting (home, clinic, community)
   - Frequency of supervision
   - Parent training schedule

### Example Treatment Goal:
"[Client] will initiate communication with peers by asking questions or making comments during unstructured play in 8 out of 10 opportunities across 3 consecutive sessions by [date]."

## Service Coverage Details

### Covered Services:
- Assessment and reassessment
- Individual ABA therapy
- Group ABA therapy (2:1 or 3:1 ratio)
- Parent/caregiver training
- BCBA supervision and oversight
- Treatment plan development and updates

### Hour Limits:
TRICARE does not impose specific hour limits but requires:
- Services to be medically necessary
- Hours justified by clinical need
- Regular progress toward goals
- Appropriate to beneficiary's needs

### Service Settings:
- Home
- Clinic/center
- School (non-duplicative of IEP services)
- Community settings

## Supervision Requirements

### BCBA Supervision Standards:
- Initial assessment within 2 weeks of service start
- Minimum 2 face-to-face contacts per month
- Supervision includes:
  - Direct observation of therapy
  - Review of data and progress
  - Treatment plan updates
  - Staff training and feedback
  - Parent consultation

### Documentation of Supervision:
- Date and duration of supervision
- Activities completed
- Progress review
- Plan modifications
- Next steps

## Progress Monitoring

### Documentation Requirements:

1. **Daily Session Notes**
   - Date and duration of service
   - Goals addressed
   - Data collected
   - Interventions used
   - Client response
   - Any concerns or observations

2. **Weekly Progress Summary**
   - Data trends
   - Skill acquisition
   - Behavior changes
   - Upcoming focus areas

3. **Monthly Progress Reports**
   - Data graphs for each goal
   - Percentage toward goal mastery
   - Barriers to progress
   - Plan modifications
   - Continued medical necessity

4. **Quarterly Comprehensive Reports**
   - Overall progress review
   - Comparison to baseline
   - Updated assessment data
   - Goal modifications
   - Hour recommendations
   - Treatment plan updates

## Parent/Caregiver Training

### Requirements:
- Parent training is a covered service
- Recommended minimum: 2-4 hours per month
- Can be delivered in various formats:
  - During therapy sessions
  - Separate training sessions
  - Home consultations
  - Group parent training

### Training Documentation:
- Training goals and objectives
- Topics covered
- Parent implementation
- Generalization data
- Parent feedback

### Acceptable Training Topics:
- Behavior management strategies
- Communication techniques
- Natural environment teaching
- Data collection
- Reinforcement strategies
- Crisis prevention and intervention

## Billing and Claims

### CPT Codes for ABA Services:
- 97151: Initial assessment
- 97152: Reassessment
- 97153: Adaptive behavior treatment by technician (15 min)
- 97155: Adaptive behavior treatment by BCBA (15 min)
- 97156: Family/caregiver training (15 min)
- 97157: Multiple family group training (15 min)
- 97158: Group adaptive behavior treatment (15 min)

### Claims Submission:
- Submit through TRICARE claims system
- Include diagnosis code F84.0
- Use appropriate CPT codes
- Document medical necessity
- Submit within filing deadline (1 year from date of service)

## Quality Standards

### TRICARE Quality Requirements:
- Follow BACB Professional and Ethical Compliance Code
- Implement evidence-based practices
- Maintain treatment fidelity
- Collect and analyze data systematically
- Modify treatment based on data
- Coordinate care with other providers

### Required Documentation:
- Signed informed consent
- Privacy practices acknowledgment
- Treatment plan approval from caregiver
- Progress reports shared with family
- Coordination of care documentation

## Medical Necessity

### TRICARE Defines Medical Necessity as:
- Treatment addresses deficits directly related to ASD
- Services are evidence-based
- Provided by qualified professionals
- Expected to result in meaningful improvement
- Cannot be achieved through less intensive means

### Documentation Must Include:
- How ASD impacts daily functioning
- Specific behaviors or skills targeted
- Why this level of intervention is needed
- Safety concerns or risks
- Family capacity to participate
- Progress toward independence

## Special Populations

### Active Duty Family Members:
- May have additional coverage options
- Coordinate with Family Advocacy Program
- Consider deployment impacts
- Ensure continuity during moves (PCS)

### ECHO (Extended Care Health Option):
- May provide extended benefits
- Additional services for qualifying beneficiaries
- Requires enrollment
- May include respite care

## Transitions of Care

### When Beneficiary Relocates:
- Provide comprehensive transition report
- Include all assessment data
- Document current treatment plan
- Share progress data
- Recommend continued services
- Connect with new providers

### Transitioning to Adult Services:
- Plan ahead for age 21 transition
- Coordinate with adult service systems
- Document continued need for services
- Explore Medicaid waiver options
- Consider vocational services

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Claim Denials:
- Ensure correct diagnosis coding
- Verify provider credentials are current
- Submit complete documentation
- Appeal if services were medically necessary

### Finding Providers:
- Use TRICARE provider directory
- Contact regional contractor
- Consider telehealth options in underserved areas

### Coordination with Schools:
- Clearly differentiate services from IEP
- Document non-duplicative nature
- Maintain communication with school team
- Focus on home and community goals
