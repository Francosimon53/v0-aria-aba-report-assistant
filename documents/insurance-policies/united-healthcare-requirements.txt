# United Healthcare (UHC) ABA Therapy Requirements

## Overview

United Healthcare covers Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy for the treatment of Autism Spectrum Disorder when deemed medically necessary. Coverage varies by plan type.

## Prior Authorization Requirements

### Initial Authorization Process:
- Required before starting any ABA services
- Processing time: 10-15 business days
- Valid for specified time period (typically 6-12 months)
- Submit through UHC provider portal or fax

### Required Documentation:
1. **Diagnostic Evaluation**
   - Current psychological evaluation (within 12 months)
   - DSM-5 diagnosis: F84.0 (Autism Spectrum Disorder)
   - ADOS-2 scores (preferred)
   - Cognitive assessment results
   
2. **Functional Assessment**
   - Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales (VABS-3)
   - Adaptive Behavior Assessment System (ABAS-3)
   - Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA)
   - Baseline data collection across domains

3. **Medical Records**
   - Primary care physician notes
   - Previous intervention attempts and outcomes
   - Current medications
   - Relevant medical history

4. **Treatment Plan**
   - BCBA-developed comprehensive treatment plan
   - Specific, measurable, achievable goals
   - Recommended hours per week
   - Treatment setting and modality

## Medical Necessity Criteria

### UHC Defines Medical Necessity When:
- Treatment addresses deficits directly related to ASD diagnosis
- Interventions are evidence-based and scientifically validated
- Services follow nationally recognized guidelines (BACB)
- Treatment is provided by qualified professionals
- Expected outcomes cannot be achieved through less intensive services
- Progress is documented and monitored regularly

### Documentation Must Show:
- How ASD symptoms impair daily functioning
- Specific behaviors targeted for intervention
- Why ABA is appropriate vs. other treatments
- Safety concerns or risk factors
- Family/caregiver involvement capacity

## Treatment Goals Requirements

### Goal Writing Standards:
All goals must be written in measurable terms following this format:

**Who**: Client name
**Will do what**: Specific, observable behavior
**How well**: Measurement criteria (accuracy %)
**Under what conditions**: Setting, prompts, materials
**By when**: Target date

### Example Compliant Goal:
"Johnny will independently request preferred items using 3-4 word sentences with 80% accuracy across 3 different settings (home, clinic, school) for 3 consecutive sessions by [date]."

### Required Goal Domains:
1. **Communication/Language**
   - Expressive communication
   - Receptive language
   - Functional communication training

2. **Social Skills**
   - Peer interaction
   - Joint attention
   - Social reciprocity

3. **Daily Living Skills**
   - Self-care
   - Independence
   - Community skills

4. **Behavior Management**
   - Reduction of maladaptive behaviors
   - Replacement behavior training
   - Self-regulation skills

5. **Academic/Pre-Academic**
   - Attention to task
   - Following instructions
   - Learning readiness

## Service Intensity Guidelines

### Recommended Hours by Level of Need:
- **Mild impairment**: 10-25 hours/week
- **Moderate impairment**: 25-40 hours/week
- **Severe impairment**: Up to 40+ hours/week (requires strong justification)

### Justification for High Hours Must Include:
- Severity of symptoms
- Safety concerns
- Failed response to lower intensity
- Support available in natural environment
- Realistic goals for intensive intervention

## Provider Qualifications

### Supervising BCBA Requirements:
- Current BCB certification
- State licensure where applicable
- Liability insurance
- Credentialed with UHC network

### Direct Care Staff Requirements:
- RBT certification OR
- Bachelor's degree in related field OR
- Supervised experience with ASD population
- Training in treatment procedures
- Supervision ratio: minimum 2 contacts per month per client

## Progress Monitoring Requirements

### Session Documentation:
- Daily data collection on all active goals
- ABC data for challenging behaviors
- Duration of services provided
- Interventions used
- Client response to intervention
- Parent/caregiver involvement

### Monthly Progress Reports Should Include:
- Data graphs for each goal
- Percentage of goal completion
- Description of progress or barriers
- Any plan modifications
- Continued medical necessity statement

### Reauthorization Reports (Every 6 Months):
- Comprehensive progress review
- Updated assessment data (VABS, ABAS)
- Comparison to baseline
- Goal modifications or new goals
- Hours recommendation with justification
- Treatment plan updates

## Parent/Caregiver Training

### UHC Requirements:
- Minimum 2 hours per month of caregiver training
- Training must be documented separately from direct therapy
- Topics covered must be specified
- Caregiver implementation and generalization data
- Training goals and objectives

### Acceptable Training Topics:
- Behavior management strategies
- Communication techniques
- Data collection procedures
- Generalization strategies
- Natural environment teaching
- Crisis intervention

## Common Reasons for Denial/Reduction

### To Avoid Denial:
1. **Incomplete Documentation**
   - Ensure all required assessments are current
   - Include all baseline data
   - Submit comprehensive treatment plan

2. **Lack of Medical Necessity**
   - Clearly justify recommended hours
   - Document why less intensive services insufficient
   - Show connection between goals and ASD diagnosis

3. **Insufficient Progress**
   - Show data demonstrating positive response
   - Explain barriers and modifications made
   - Document when lack of progress due to medical complexity

4. **Non-Covered Services**
   - Ensure services are focused on ASD-related deficits
   - Avoid educational-only goals
   - Don't mix developmental delays unrelated to ASD

## Reauthorization Process

### Submit 30 Days Before Expiration:
- Updated treatment plan
- Progress report with data
- Current assessment scores
- Continued medical necessity justification
- Hours recommendation

### Required Elements:
- Comparison to previous authorization period
- Demonstration of meaningful progress
- Explanation of any regression
- Plan modifications based on progress
- Family/caregiver input and cooperation

## Appeals Process

### If Authorization Denied:
1. Review denial letter for specific reasons
2. Gather additional supporting documentation
3. Submit written appeal within 30 days
4. Include peer-reviewed research supporting intervention
5. Provide detailed clinical rationale
6. Consider requesting peer-to-peer review

### Expedited Appeals:
Available when denial creates risk to health
- Submit urgent appeal request
- Provide medical documentation of urgency
- Decision within 72 hours

## Special Considerations

### Telehealth Services:
- Covered when medically appropriate
- Same documentation requirements
- Must demonstrate effectiveness
- Parent supervision may be required

### School-Based Services:
- Cannot duplicate IEP services
- Must target goals outside educational domain
- Coordination with school staff documented
- Clear differentiation from school services

### Concurrent Services:
- Speech therapy may be concurrent
- Occupational therapy may be concurrent
- Must show non-duplication of services
- Coordination of care documented

## Quality Measures

UHC tracks quality indicators including:
- Treatment plan individualization
- Goal achievement rates
- Caregiver satisfaction
- Safety incidents
- Service utilization patterns
