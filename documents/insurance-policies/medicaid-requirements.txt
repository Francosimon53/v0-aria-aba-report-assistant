# Medicaid ABA Therapy Coverage Requirements

## Overview

Medicaid coverage for Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) varies by state. This document outlines general requirements, but providers must verify specific requirements with their state Medicaid program.

## State-Specific Coverage

### Important Note:
- Each state administers its own Medicaid program
- Coverage policies vary significantly by state
- Some states have ABA mandates, others provide discretionary coverage
- Always verify current state requirements

### Common Coverage Models:
1. **Fee-for-Service**: Direct billing to state Medicaid
2. **Managed Care**: Authorization through MCO (e.g., AmeriHealth, Molina)
3. **Waiver Programs**: HCBS waiver services
4. **EPSDT**: Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic and Treatment

## General Authorization Requirements

### Prior Authorization (Most States):
- Submit authorization request with comprehensive documentation
- Processing time: 14-30 days (varies by state)
- Authorization periods: 3-12 months
- Reauthorization required at specified intervals

### Required Documentation:

1. **Diagnostic Documentation**
   - Comprehensive psychological evaluation
   - DSM-5 diagnosis: F84.0 (Autism Spectrum Disorder)
   - Developmental assessment
   - Cognitive evaluation
   - Completed within past 12 months

2. **Functional Assessment**
   - Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales (VABS-3)
   - ABLLS-R or VB-MAPP (many states prefer)
   - Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA)
   - Baseline data across all target areas

3. **Medical Necessity Documentation**
   - Physician referral or prescription
   - Statement of medical necessity
   - Description of functional impairments
   - Justification for recommended hours

4. **Treatment Plan**
   - BCBA-developed comprehensive plan
   - Measurable goals and objectives
   - Intervention strategies
   - Service delivery plan
   - Parent training component

## EPSDT Requirements

### Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic, and Treatment:
Medicaid must cover medically necessary services for children under 21, including:
- Screening services
- Diagnostic services
- Treatment services (including ABA)

### Key EPSDT Principles:
- Must correct or ameliorate conditions
- Broader coverage than adult services
- Cannot be limited solely by state plan
- Must consider child's developmental needs

## Medical Necessity Criteria

### Medicaid Generally Requires:
- Treatment addresses deficits directly related to ASD diagnosis
- Services are evidence-based
- Expected to result in significant improvement
- Provided by qualified professionals
- Cannot be achieved through less intensive services
- Age-appropriate interventions

### Documentation Must Demonstrate:
- How ASD impacts daily functioning and safety
- Why ABA is necessary vs. other interventions
- Expected outcomes with timeframe
- Family support and involvement
- Barriers addressed in treatment plan

## Treatment Plan Requirements

### Comprehensive Treatment Plan Must Include:

1. **Client Profile**
   - Demographics and diagnosis
   - Current functioning levels
   - Strengths and preferences
   - Family priorities

2. **Assessment Results**
   - Standardized assessment scores
   - Functional assessment findings
   - Baseline data summary
   - Skill deficits and excesses

3. **Treatment Goals**
   - Minimum 3-5 measurable goals
   - SMART goal format
   - Across multiple domains
   - Prioritized with family input

4. **Intervention Procedures**
   - Evidence-based strategies
   - Teaching procedures
   - Behavior reduction plans
   - Reinforcement systems

5. **Service Specifications**
   - Recommended hours per week
   - Service settings
   - Staff qualifications
   - Supervision plan
   - Parent training schedule

### Goal Writing Requirements:
Goals must be objective, measurable, and include:
- Target behavior
- Measurement criteria
- Conditions
- Timeline

**Example:** "[Child] will independently use a minimum of 50 different functional words to request, comment, and label across 2 different settings with 80% accuracy for 3 consecutive weeks by [date]."

## Service Coverage

### Typically Covered Services:
- Initial comprehensive assessment
- Treatment plan development
- Direct 1:1 therapy
- Group therapy (varies by state)
- Parent/caregiver training
- BCBA supervision
- Periodic reassessments

### Hour Limitations:
- Varies significantly by state
- Ranges from 15-40 hours per week typical
- Higher hours require strong justification
- May require step-down over time

### Service Settings:
- Home (most common)
- Clinic/center
- Community settings
- School (if non-duplicative of IEP)

## Provider Qualifications

### BCBA Requirements:
- Current Board Certification
- State licensure where required
- Medicaid provider enrollment
- Background checks completed
- Liability insurance

### RBT/Technician Requirements:
- RBT certification (required in many states)
- High school diploma minimum
- Completed competency assessment
- Ongoing supervision by BCBA

### Supervision Ratios:
- Typically 1 BCBA to 10-16 clients
- Minimum 2 contacts per client per month
- 5-10% of direct service hours

## Progress Monitoring

### Daily Documentation:
- Session date and duration
- Goals addressed
- Data collected
- Interventions implemented
- Client response
- Any incidents or concerns

### Progress Reports:

**Monthly:**
- Data graphs for each goal
- Progress summary
- Barriers identified
- Plan modifications

**Quarterly:**
- Comprehensive progress review
- Comparison to baseline
- Updated assessment data
- Goal modifications
- Treatment plan updates

**Annual:**
- Full reassessment
- Comprehensive progress report
- Long-term outcomes
- Recommendations for continued services

## Parent/Caregiver Training

### Requirements:
- Must be included in treatment plan
- Recommended: 2-4 hours per month minimum
- Documented as separate service
- Billable component

### Training Components:
- Behavior management strategies
- Communication techniques
- Data collection
- Generalization strategies
- Natural environment teaching
- Self-care and safety skills

### Documentation:
- Training goals and objectives
- Topics covered
- Parent implementation data
- Progress toward training goals

## Billing and Reimbursement

### Common CPT Codes:
- 97151: Initial assessment
- 97152: Reassessment
- 97153: Adaptive behavior treatment by technician
- 97155: Adaptive behavior treatment by BCBA
- 97156: Family/caregiver training
- 0362T: Group therapy (if covered)

### Billing Requirements:
- Use correct Medicaid ID
- Include appropriate diagnosis code
- Submit within timely filing limits
- Maintain detailed documentation
- Include modifiers when required

### Reimbursement Rates:
- Vary significantly by state
- Typically lower than commercial insurance
- May require contracted rates
- Some states use global rates

## Managed Care Organizations (MCOs)

### When Beneficiary in MCO:
- Authorization through MCO, not state Medicaid
- Follow MCO-specific procedures
- May have different requirements
- Separate provider credentialing needed

### Common Medicaid MCOs:
- UnitedHealthcare Community Plan
- Molina Healthcare
- Centene/WellCare
- AmeriHealth Caritas
- Blue Cross Blue Shield Community Plans

## Waiver Programs

### Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) Waivers:
- Additional services beyond state plan
- Autism-specific waivers in some states
- May cover respite, parent training, behavioral consultation
- Separate authorization process

### Katie Beckett/TEFRA Waivers:
- For children with disabilities
- May provide enhanced coverage
- Family income not considered
- Supports keeping children at home

## Special Considerations

### Coordination with Schools:
- Must not duplicate IEP services
- Focus on home and community skills
- Document non-duplication
- Coordinate care meetings recommended

### Concurrent Services:
- Speech therapy typically concurrent
- Occupational therapy typically concurrent
- Physical therapy as needed
- Coordination of care required

### Telehealth:
- Many states expanded coverage during COVID
- Requirements vary by state
- May require parent supervision
- Same documentation standards

## Common Denial Reasons

### How to Avoid Denials:

1. **Incomplete Documentation**
   - Submit all required assessments
   - Include comprehensive baseline data
   - Provide detailed treatment plan

2. **Lack of Medical Necessity**
   - Clearly justify recommended hours
   - Document why less intensive insufficient
   - Show expected functional improvements

3. **Provider Issues**
   - Ensure enrollment current
   - Verify credentials up-to-date
   - Use correct billing codes

4. **Insufficient Progress**
   - Document progress with data
   - Explain barriers to progress
   - Show treatment modifications

## Appeals Process

### If Authorization Denied:

1. **Review Denial Letter**
   - Note specific reasons
   - Identify missing information
   - Check appeal deadline

2. **Submit Appeal**
   - Typically 30-60 days to appeal
   - Include additional documentation
   - Provide clinical rationale
   - Request peer review if available

3. **Fair Hearing**
   - Available if appeal denied
   - State hearing process
   - Legal representation allowed
   - Administrative law judge decides
