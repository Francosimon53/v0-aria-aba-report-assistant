# Cigna ABA Therapy Coverage Requirements

## Prior Authorization Process

Cigna requires prior authorization for all ABA therapy services. The authorization process must be completed before services begin.

### Required Documentation for Authorization:
- Comprehensive psychological evaluation completed within the last 12 months
- DSM-5 diagnosis code (F84.0 for Autism Spectrum Disorder)
- Adaptive Behavior Assessment System (ABAS-3) scores
- Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales scores
- BCBA recommendation letter
- Treatment plan with specific, measurable goals
- Medical necessity justification

### Authorization Timeline:
- Initial authorization: Up to 6 months of treatment
- Reauthorization required every 6 months
- Submit reauthorization requests 30 days before expiration

## Assessment Requirements

### Initial Assessment Must Include:
1. **Behavioral Assessment**
   - Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA)
   - ABC (Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence) data
   - Baseline data across all targeted skills
   - Environmental assessment

2. **Developmental Assessment**
   - Cognitive functioning evaluation
   - Language and communication assessment
   - Social skills evaluation
   - Motor skills assessment

3. **Adaptive Functioning**
   - Self-care skills
   - Domestic skills
   - Community participation
   - Social interaction abilities

## Treatment Plan Requirements

### Goals Must Be:
- Specific and clearly defined
- Measurable with objective criteria
- Achievable within the authorization period
- Relevant to the client's diagnosis and needs
- Time-bound with target dates

### Example Goal Format:
"[Client name] will independently request preferred items using 2-3 word phrases in 8 out of 10 opportunities across 3 consecutive sessions by [target date]."

### Required Goal Categories:
1. Communication skills
2. Social interaction skills
3. Adaptive living skills
4. Behavior reduction (if applicable)
5. Parent/caregiver training

## Service Delivery Specifications

### Coverage Details:
- Maximum hours per week: Varies by medical necessity (typically 20-40 hours)
- Setting: Home, clinic, school, or community
- Provider qualifications: BCBA supervision required
- RBT/technician ratio: Must comply with BACB requirements

### Documentation Requirements:
- Daily session notes required
- Weekly progress summaries
- Monthly data graphs showing progress
- Quarterly comprehensive reports

## Progress Reporting

### Monthly Reports Must Include:
- Current data on all treatment goals
- Progress toward mastery criteria
- Barriers to progress
- Plan modifications if needed
- Continued medical necessity justification

### Quarterly Reports Must Include:
- Comprehensive review of all goals
- Updated assessment data
- Treatment plan modifications
- Recommendations for continued services
- Caregiver training documentation

## Medical Necessity Criteria

Cigna defines medical necessity for ABA as treatment that:
- Addresses skill deficits directly related to autism diagnosis
- Is evidence-based and follows BACB guidelines
- Shows measurable progress toward treatment goals
- Cannot be provided through less intensive services
- Is delivered by qualified professionals

### Key Documentation Points:
- Describe how autism symptoms impact daily functioning
- Explain why ABA is necessary vs. other interventions
- Document failed attempts at less intensive treatments
- Highlight safety concerns if applicable
- Demonstrate progress with data

## Caregiver Training Requirements

Cigna requires documentation of parent/caregiver training:
- Minimum 2 hours per month of caregiver training
- Training topics documented
- Caregiver implementation data
- Generalization of skills to home environment

## Discharge Criteria

Treatment may be discontinued when:
- Client has met all treatment goals
- Client shows no progress over 6 months despite plan modifications
- Client no longer meets medical necessity criteria
- Caregiver requests discontinuation
- Services are no longer appropriate for the client's needs

## Common Denial Reasons

To avoid denials, ensure:
- All assessments are current (within 12 months)
- Goals are measurable and autism-specific
- Medical necessity is clearly documented
- Progress data is included with every authorization
- BCBA credentials are current and documented
